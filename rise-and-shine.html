Rise&Shine

Overview

Rise&Shine is a smart alarm app concept created for a university assignment focused on improving morning routines. The project explored how combining alarms with essential morning information, such as weather and reminders, could help users start their day with more clarity and less stress.

In this project, I collaborated with two other students and contributed to user interviews, early sketching, wireframing, UI design, and lightweight testing. As my first UX design experience, Rise&Shine taught me essential skills in conducting early discovery, organizing research findings, planning design directions, and creating initial interface concepts.

Problem statement

Most alarm apps focus only on basic functionality, offering little support for the overall morning routine.

Users often feel rushed or stressed when starting their day.

Relevant information (weather, reminders, commute details) is fragmented across multiple apps.

Project Goals

Support a calmer and more organized start to the day.

Create an interface that feels intuitive, minimal, and easy to use first thing in the morning.

Explore how personalization can help users prepare for their day most effectively.

Bring essential morning information into one unified experience.

Research
Persona

After defining the key insights, we developed a persona that represents the most common patterns, motivations, and behaviors observed during the research. This helped ground our design decisions in the needs of a realistic user rather than assumptions.

About

Anna is a university student who tends to go to bed late and wake up early, often leaving many tasks for the morning. She relies heavily on her phone to organize her day and prefers a gentle, predictable start rather than rushing immediately after waking up.

Needs

She needs a gentle, low‑effort wake‑up experience paired with a single, unified space where she can see the most important morning details. Clear, simple guidance helps her feel more grounded when she starts her day.

Goals

Wants to start her mornings calmly and feel more in control of her routine. She aims to quickly access essential information — such as weather, news, and reminders — without jumping between multiple apps.

Frustrations

She often uses multiple snoozes, which makes her mornings feel disorganized. Moving between different apps for basic information takes extra time and adds unnecessary friction to the first minutes of her day.

Design
Design principles

To translate the user's needs into a structured interaction, I mapped the two core flows that occur inside the app: setting an alarm and starting the morning after waking up. These flows helped clarify how the experience should stay simple and low-effort.

Create a dismissal interaction with the right level of friction — hard enough to fully wake up, easy enough not to frustrate.

Prioritize simplicity and reduce cognitive load for users who are just waking up.

Integrate essential morning info into one clean overview — weather, tasks, and commute at a glance.

Support morning routines with structured and easy-to-follow checklists.

Final Design

Four core screens covering the complete morning experience — alarm dismissal, weather overview, daily tasks, and AI-powered morning tips.

Key design decisions
Drag-and-drop alarm dismissal

The drag button changes position with each alarm to ensure the user is fully awake before dismissing — adding the right amount of intentional friction to prevent accidental snoozing.

Weekday vs. weekend task separation

Different task lists for weekdays and weekends allow users to maintain distinct routines without cluttering a single shared list, supporting a more personalized and organized experience.

Unified morning overview

Weather, forecast, activity conditions, and reminders are brought together in one screen — removing the need to switch between apps in the first groggy minutes of the day.

Personalized AI tips

Users can select morning tip categories (clothing, coffee, commute, podcasts) and even use voice input — making the experience feel tailored without requiring much effort to set up.

Reflection
What I learned

Rise&Shine was my first real UX project, and it shaped how I think about design. Talking to users early — before touching the interface — showed me how different real needs are from assumptions. I learned that good UX isn't about adding features; it's about removing friction in the moments that matter most.

Working with a team also taught me how to communicate design reasoning, align on direction, and iterate together. The experience gave me a foundation I've built on in every project since.
